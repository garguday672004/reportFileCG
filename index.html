CHALLENGES

Grappling with Mathematical Functions

During the development of our computer graphics project, one of the primary challenges we encountered was understanding and effectively utilizing complex mathematical functions within the codebase. As students with varying levels of mathematical proficiency, navigating functions such as sin(), cos(), and hypot() proved to be a daunting task. Our goal was to comprehend how these mathematical functions contributed to the generation of dynamic visual elements and animations within the project. However, the abstract nature of mathematical concepts and their application in programming posed significant hurdles. To overcome this challenge, we embarked on a journey of self-directed learning and collaboration. We engaged in extensive research, seeking out tutorials, articles, and online resources to deepen our understanding of mathematical functions and their role in computer graphics. Furthermore, we leveraged peer support and group study sessions to tackle difficult concepts collectively. Through collaborative problem-solving and hands-on experimentation, we gradually gained confidence in manipulating mathematical functions to achieve desired visual effects. By persevering through the challenges posed by mathematical functions, we not only enhanced our understanding of computer graphics principles but also developed valuable problem-solving skills essential for future projects.
